Navigating Retro Soundscape

Playing Sounds

To start playing a sound, click the Start/Stop button at the top of the page, and press the button again to stop the sound.

You can play different musical notes by pressing different keys on the keyboard - check the key bindings on the right side of the page.

Use the right and left arrow keys to go up or down an octave on the musical scale

Change the scale of the notes played by entering the letter of the scale you would like to play into the Musial Scale field. Use capitals for major keys and lowercase for minor keys.

Ex: to play in D major, enter and submit "D", to play F minor, enter "f".

Saving Sounds

Once a sound is playing, to keep it playing while creating another, click the Save Sound button on the right side of the screen.

Once this is done, press the Start/Stop button to make a new sound.

To Delete a saved sound, simply press the Delete button, and the most recently saved soundwave will stop playing

Editing Sound and Visuals

Sliders:

  • Volume: Controls the volume of the current soundwave
  • Distortion: Controls the distortion of the current soundwave
  • Flatline Rotation: Controls the rotation of the visuals when in "Flatline" mode
  • VertSlider: Offsets vertical placement of "Flatline" visuals and makes "Droopy Mountain" visuals a little squiggly

The sine wave button controls the wave type of the soundwave, and clicking it will go through four options for a wave: sine, sawtooth, triangle, and square

The Droopy Mountain button changes the visual representation of the waves from being with rectangles, to with elipses

Key Bindings

The default key bindings are set up for the keys: [z, x, c, v, m, ,, ., /]

To change that, enter in the eight new keys to bind the notes to in the provided space separated by only whitespace.

An example input would look like this:
a s d f k l ; '
